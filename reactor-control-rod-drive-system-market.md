<p><p>The Reactor Control Rod Drive System market consists of several types tailored to different reactor designs. Pressurized Water Reactors (PWR) use control rods to manage neutron flow effectively, while Boiling Water Reactors (BWR) manipulate coolant boiling to control reactivity. Heavy Water Reactors (HWR) utilize deuterium in the coolant for enhanced efficiency. Graphite Gas-Cooled Reactors rely on graphite moderators and gas coolant, whereas Graphite Water-Cooled Reactors combine graphite moderation with water cooling. Fast Breeder Reactors are designed for high-speed neutron use to generate fuel, each requiring specialized control systems.</p></p>
